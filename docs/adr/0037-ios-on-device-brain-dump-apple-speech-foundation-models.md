# iOS on-device brain dump: Apple Speech transcription + Foundation Models extraction, with salvage-draft fallback

**Status:** accepted (sibling to ADR-0029 for the native macOS app; advances ADR-0027, which deferred iOS on-device inference "with its app").

**Context.** The shared brain-dump substrate already exists and is iOS-capable — the `SpeechToText` port + `SpeechToTextSelector` (ADR-0018), the `Extractor` + `InferenceEngine` seam (ADR-0027), the `BrainDumpComponent` + Inbox, and the SwiftUI capture overlay. iOS was the one platform with **no real engine actuals**: `core/speech` `appleMain` binds the `UnavailableSpeechToText` floor and inference resolves to NotConfigured, so an iOS brain dump records audio that goes nowhere. The cloud relay is off-device (explicit opt-in + entitlement) and blocked anyway (relay PAT unconfigured, Deferno#345); whisper-on-iOS (ADR-0018/0019) needs cinterop + a ~182 MB On-Demand-Resource model. Meanwhile **iOS 26** ships two on-device Apple frameworks that fit exactly: the `Speech` framework's `SpeechAnalyzer`/`SpeechTranscriber` (long-form on-device STT) and `FoundationModels` (on-device LLM, Apple-Intelligence hardware).

**Decision.** Build iOS brain dump fully **on-device** with Apple frameworks, mirroring the native macOS app's in-process Swift→Kotlin pattern (ADR-0029): Swift capabilities in `app/iosApp/iosApp/`, Kotlin/Native bridges in `iosMain`, injected through `DefernoRoot` into the DI graph. `core/speech` `appleMain` keeps `UnavailableSpeechToText` as the floor; the real engines register from the app layer (as macOS does with `NativeSpeechToText`/`NativeInference`).

- **STT — two Apple engines into the existing ranked selector.** `SpeechTranscriber` (iOS 26, long-form, rank high) is primary; `SFSpeechRecognizer` (`requiresOnDeviceRecognition`, broad-device, rank low) is the fallback. The `SpeechToTextSelector` (ADR-0018) picks the highest available, so streaming New-form dictation works down to older iOS via the fallback, while the brain-dump worker transcribes the recorded WAV with `SpeechTranscriber` (iOS 26 only — fine, since extraction pins iOS 26 regardless). `availability()` reports not-ready until the per-locale `SpeechTranscriber` asset is installed.
- **Extraction — Apple Foundation Models as an on-device `InferenceEngine`.** A new iOS `InferenceEngine` actual calls a Swift `FoundationModels` callback and decodes its JSON against the Kotlin `InferenceSchema`, so schema + validation stay Kotlin-owned and the propose-only boundary (ADR-0027/0029) holds. It registers as an on-device catalog engine and is the **default** on iOS (per the ADR-0027 amendment: on-device defaults ON). It reports available only when `SystemLanguageModel` is available (iOS 26 + Apple-Intelligence hardware).
- **Shared pipeline + salvage.** The Android worker and the new iOS runner both delegate to a shared `commonMain` pipeline `transcribe → extract → (persist drafts | Salvage draft)`, with transcription, extraction, audio-retention, and notification as injected platform seams. Every non-empty take yields something (CONTEXT.md *Salvage draft*); reason strings are identical cross-platform. A trivially-empty take (accidental tap) is dropped at the recorder seam, not salvaged.
- **Background execution — layered, durable, no BGTask dependency for the common case.** On Stop the WAV is finalized into a durable `pending/` dir; processing runs on an app-scope coroutine (survives the overlay dismissing) inside `UIApplication.beginBackgroundTask` for a grace window; a relaunch sweep re-processes leftover WAVs (covers app death); a `BGProcessingTask` backstop finishes a take while the app is away. A single idempotent per-WAV claim coordinates the three so nothing double-processes.
- **Notification — opt-in App setting (default off), shared with Android.** Off → drafts just appear in the Inbox; on → a local notification (tap opens the Inbox), requesting iOS notification authorization at opt-in.

**Considered and rejected.** *whisper.xcframework + ODR model* (ADR-0018's portable floor) — heavier (cinterop, ~182 MB On-Demand Resource, model-readiness gating) and, given the iOS 26 + Apple-Intelligence floor extraction already forces, buys nothing over native long-form `SpeechTranscriber`; retained as a future portability floor only. *`SFSpeechRecognizer` alone* — short-utterance-oriented, the weak case for long-form brain dump (ADR-0018 ranks whisper above it for long audio); kept only as the broad-compatibility fallback for short dictation. *Cloud relay extraction* — off-device (explicit opt-in + entitlement) and blocked on Deferno#345; remains the future fallback for non-Apple-Intelligence devices. *Per-feature AI opt-in prompt* — dropped for a default-on App setting + onboarding consent (ADR-0027 amendment). *BGTaskScheduler as the primary path* — OS-deferred timing (minutes–hours) is wrong for immediate draft turnaround; demoted to a backstop.

**Consequences.** End-to-end iOS brain-dump drafts require **iOS 26 + Apple-Intelligence hardware**; everywhere else it degrades to a Salvage draft (and, later, the cloud relay). New Info.plist usage strings (microphone + speech recognition) and the SwiftUI-owned Preflight (speech-then-mic, #120) apply. The iOS `InferenceEnginePreference` moves from in-memory to `NSUserDefaults`-backed (the swap its own doc comment already anticipates). Android's brain-dump worker is refactored onto the shared pipeline — a deliberate behavior change: salvage replaces notify-0-and-discard, and the completion notification becomes opt-in. The Apple `FoundationModels`/`Speech` boundaries sit behind fake-able seams (like the macOS `NativeInference`) to stay testable under the merged coverage gate.
